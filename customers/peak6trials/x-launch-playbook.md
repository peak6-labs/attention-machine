## Core Principle

**Novelty over incrementalism.** If not novel, won't work on X. Products should be positioned with the boldest claim you can still defend.

- Weak: "helps your brand get recommended more"
- Strong: "world's first AI SEO team to get your brand ranked number one by ChatGPT"

---

## Launch Video Structure

1. **Opening** — Lead with the biggest defensible claim within the first 5 seconds
2. **Demonstration** — Show the product working in real-time with 3–5 "wow moments." Demonstrations begin within 10 seconds, use present tense narration ("Right now, the system is scanning...")
3. **Social Proof** — Keep to one line if the founder is unknown
4. **Lead Magnet** — Require retweets and comments to capture emails
5. **Subtle Controversy** — Include one edge element targeting specific communities

### Video Production Standards

- Follow "Mr Beast standard" — audio and visual match one-to-one
- Write at a fifth-grade reading level for maximum clarity
- Use present tense, not past tense
- No technical jargon or vague outcomes

---

## Algorithm Mechanics

- **Retweets** determine initial distribution (sourcing signal)
- **Replies** determine ranking — reply chains boost ranking when authors respond back
- Controversy elements targeting specific communities drive engagement
- Follow-up content sustains momentum beyond launch day

---

## Influencer Campaign

- Budget: **$500–$3,000 per influencer** across 30–100 creators
- **Never let influencers write their own posts** — study their pages extensively and craft custom content matching their voice
- Focus on **engagement rates over follower counts**
- Target **niche business owners over traditional influencers**
- Deploy in staggered waves over 2–4 hours, not simultaneously
- Monitor real-time momentum and adjust

### Five Proven Influencer Formats

1. Five-ways posts
2. Comparisons
3. Founder stories
4. Giveaways
5. Listicles

---

## 10 Best Tricks

- Include giveaways in launch videos requiring specific engagement (retweets + comments)
- Add subtle controversial elements to trigger engagement
- Reply to ALL comments — described as a "massive miss" if overlooked
- Adapt proven viral content rather than creating original formats
- Time amplification waves strategically over 24–48 hours
- Copy proven content formats rather than innovating formats

---

## Distribution Timeline

1. **Phase 1** — Allow 30–60 minutes of organic growth before amplification
2. **Phase 2** — Deploy 30–100 influencers over staggered 30-minute windows
3. **Real-time adjustment** — Monitor engagement and deploy reserves throughout
4. **Post-launch follow-up** — Next day: memes, deep dives, testimonials to ride algorithm momentum from viral launch

---

## Implementation Phases

1. **Pre-launch research** (10–20 hours analyzing viral formats) — Identify 20+ viral post examples with 500+ likes, 50+ comments, 50+ reposts
2. **Production** — Video optimized for clarity and speed
3. **Influencer coordination** — Staggered posting across 24–48 hours
4. **Real-time adjustment** — Monitoring engagement and deploying reserves
5. **Post-launch follow-up** — Memes, deep dives, testimonials

Detailed checklists span six weeks pre-launch through one week post-launch.

---

## Key Mistakes to Avoid

- Using past tense instead of present demonstrations
- Featuring technical jargon or vague outcomes
- Neglecting to reply to comments
- Deploying amplification too early
- Letting campaigns end after one day
- Letting influencers write their own content

---

## Success Metrics

- Target: 2–10M views
- 50–200+ qualified demos from launch campaigns

## Copywriting Techniques

- Use intensity prompts: "make this more novel," "turn up the intensity"
