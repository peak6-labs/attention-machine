# X Engagement

A playbook for engaging on X/Twitter — how to participate in conversations
effectively. Apply this skill whenever you're replying, quote-tweeting,
building a thread, or deciding whether to engage at all.

## When to use

- Deciding whether to reply to a conversation
- Choosing between reply, quote-tweet, or thread
- Timing engagement for maximum impact
- Evaluating whether PEAK6 should participate at all

## When NOT to use

- Drafting standalone content (use `x-content-doctrine`)
- Researching a topic (use `x-research`)

## Engage or ignore?

Before engaging, answer three questions:

1. **Does PEAK6 have genuine expertise here?** Not just an opinion — actual
   domain knowledge to contribute. "+1" and "great point" are not engagement.
2. **Is the conversation still active?** < 24h old with recent replies = yes.
   Stale threads (> 24h, no new activity) = generally no.
3. **Is this a conversation we want to be associated with?** Check topic
   boundaries from `x-content-doctrine`. If the topic is off-limits or the
   thread is hostile, walk away.

If any answer is no, **don't engage**. Silence is better than weak engagement.

### Always ignore

- Hostile, inflammatory, or bait threads
- Topics on the off-limits list (specific positions, politics, crypto prices)
- Authors in the excluded handles list
- Threads where PEAK6 has no unique angle
- Conversations older than 48 hours

### Always investigate

- Direct PEAK6 mentions (@ tags or name references)
- Authority handles asking questions in PEAK6 domains
- Threads with 100+ engagements on PEAK6 domain topics
- Clusters (3+ authors on same topic)

## Choosing engagement type

### Reply

Use when:
- Adding a specific insight to an existing conversation
- Answering a question you have genuine expertise on
- The original author has > 1,000 followers or is in an authority list
- Your response is 1-3 tweets (if longer, consider a quote-tweet or thread)

Reply guidelines:
- **Lead with the insight**, not with agreement. "Here's what we're seeing..."
  not "Great point! We also think..."
- **Match the energy** of the thread. Serious thread → serious reply. Casual
  thread → conversational reply.
- **Never tag-dump or hijack** threads for visibility
- **Get conversation context first** — use `get-thread` before replying to
  understand the full discussion, not just the tweet you're replying to

### Quote-tweet

Use when:
- Adding substantial commentary (3+ sentences of original thought)
- The original tweet sets up a take PEAK6 can meaningfully expand
- You want to surface the conversation to PEAK6's audience
- The original author would benefit from the amplification

Quote-tweet guidelines:
- The quote must add value beyond the original. If you're just agreeing,
  reply instead.
- Frame the quote — tell your audience why this matters
- Don't use quote-tweets to argue. If you disagree, reply directly or
  don't engage.

### Thread

Use when:
- You have a multi-part insight (3-7 tweets)
- A topic deserves deeper exploration than a single tweet
- You're building a narrative (story, analysis, walkthrough)

Thread guidelines:
- First tweet must stand alone as a hook — clear claim or question
- Each tweet independently readable
- Number explicitly: (1/5), (2/5), etc.
- 3-7 tweets. If it needs 10+, it's a blog post.
- End with a clear takeaway or call-to-action

### Amplification (repost)

Use when:
- The content aligns with PEAK6 pillars and needs no additional commentary
- Boosting an authority or partner's visibility
- Low effort, high signal (strong content that speaks for itself)

Use sparingly. Original content > amplification.

## Timing and velocity

**The first 30 minutes matter most.** X's algorithm heavily rewards early
engagement — a post that generates a 15-reply conversation in the first hour
gets dramatically more distribution than one that gets 15 likes over a day.

### Tactical timing

- **Reply to trending threads quickly** — engagement value decays fast
- **Coordinate hub/spoke** — when the hub account posts, spoke accounts
  (builder voice) should be ready to reply and vice versa. This creates
  thread depth that the algorithm rewards.
- **Don't batch engagement** — spacing out 5 replies over an hour is better
  than dropping 5 replies in 2 minutes (looks automated)

### Best posting windows (US Eastern)

- **Morning** (7-9 AM) — Finance/markets audience is active
- **Midday** (11 AM-1 PM) — Broad audience, good for general fintech
- **Evening** (5-7 PM) — Tech/startup audience browsing after work

These are guidelines. Authority conversations happen any time — if a
high-signal thread is active at 10 PM, engage then.

## Tone matching

| Thread energy | Your approach |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Technical/analytical** | Data-driven, cite specifics, show domain depth |
| **Casual/exploratory** | Conversational, share perspective, ask follow-up questions |
| **Breaking news** | Factual, timely, avoid speculation |
| **Debate/disagreement** | Respectful, evidence-based, acknowledge the other position |
| **Celebratory** | Warm, genuine, brief. No corporate congratulations. |

**Never**:
- Use a formal tone in a casual thread
- Be casual about serious topics (regulation, compliance, market events)
- Argue or be snarky, even if the other party is
- Use emojis from the hub account in serious contexts
